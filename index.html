Hi. I am Luís Alves. A young software developer looking for a first job.

My passion for programming started with RoboParty 2014, a robot building competition based on the Bot n Roll ONE A, an Arduino compatible board. We were divided in groups and I was more interested in the programming process than the building itself. It was my first real exposure to the logical world of programming and the moment I decided to pursue this passion.

A few months later, I was enrolling at Faculdade de Engenharia da Universidade do Porto (FEUP), in an Integrated Master in Informatics and Computing Engineering . There my horizons were vastly expanded, learning both what it means to be a software developer and an engineer.

In the meantime, I had been very active in the world of organizations, volunteering in a local association in my hometown - Associação Juvenil de Deão (AJD) - where I eventually became president, as well as at the federation of associations for my district. We provided activities, classes, and workshops for the local community, and we developed projects in several areas such as civility, equality, inclusivity, and environment. Some by ourselves, some in partnership with the federation and other organizations in the area, and some in an international level through the Erasmus+ programme. I was particularly involved in the latter, talking to international partners, applying to grants, and traveling a bit all over Europe.

Upon reaching the necessary credits to achieve a Bachelor's Degree, I decided to take a break and volunteer abroad through the European Solidarity Corps. I was deployed to Razlog, Bulgaria, where I joined the team at International Initiatives for Cooperation.

At the moment, I find myself more inclined to get a job, gain much needed experience, and start building my life. I plan on finishing my Master's eventually, but I don't think it's what I need right now.
